Breakfast Is Dumb

The health benefits of breakfast meal were always a debate. There's a lot of negative and positive correlation as this BBC article points out eating breakfast could help with weight control and with control of metabolism and diabetes:

Eating breakfast, the researchers conclude, is essential for keeping our body clock running on time.

That's n=36 study so I'd really take it with a huge grain of salt.
That being said ok, maybe breakfast can be "healthy" but there are many more ways to approach metabolism and weight control, so this argued benefits seems to be very minor - let me argue against it and say that negatives of breakfast way outweigh the positives.

Aging

Latest trends in aging studies suggest that loss of information in our epigenome (part of DNA that tells cell what should it become) causes aging. A brilliant video by [Veratasium][veratasium] covers this really well:

To summarize bits related to this topic:

To counter decline of epigenome, we can activate the body's own defenses against aging by stressing the body. Eat less, eat less protein, engage in intense exercise, experience uncomfortable cold. When the body senses existential threats it triggers longevity genes, which attempt to maintain the body to ensure its survival until good times return. This may be the evolutionary legacy of early bacteria, which established these two modes of living (repair and protect vs grow and reproduce).
Scientists are uncovering ways to mimic stresses on the body without the discomfort of fasting. Molecules like NMN also trigger sirtuins to monitor and repair the epigenome. This may slow aging.

The idea that stress on the body decreases aging is definitely not a new one and you'd probably find plenty of anecdotes in your life yourself!
